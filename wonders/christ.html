Christ the Redeemer — Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

The towering symbol of faith overlooking Rio’s bay.

About

Overlooking Rio de Janeiro from Mount Corcovado, the Christ the Redeemer statue is one of the most recognizable monuments in the world. Built between 1922 and 1931, this 30-meter-tall Art Deco masterpiece was the result of a collaboration between Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, French sculptor Paul Landowski, Romanian artist Gheorghe Leonida (who carved the face), and structural engineer Albert Caquot.

Symbolizing peace and faith, the statue with outstretched arms has become a cultural and spiritual icon, not only of Brazil but of Christianity worldwide. Constructed of reinforced concrete and covered in soapstone tiles, the monument has withstood decades of harsh weather and frequent lightning strikes. The summit offers breathtaking views of Rio’s beaches, forests, and favelas, making it both a pilgrimage site and a tourist attraction.

Quick Facts

  • Completed: 1931 CE
  • Height: 38 m (125 ft)
  • Location: Mount Corcovado, Rio de Janeiro
  • Material: Reinforced concrete & soapstone
  • UNESCO status: Part of Rio’s World Heritage Landscape (2012)

Did You Know?

  • The statue was built in Brazil but the head and hands were crafted in France.
  • It has been struck by lightning multiple times and is regularly repaired.
  • Visitors reach the top by train or escalator through Tijuca National Park.

FAQs

How do you get to the statue?
Take the Corcovado Train from Cosme Velho station or official vans from Largo do Machado.

When is it open?
Daily from 8 a.m. to 6 p.m.

Can I go inside the statue?
No — there’s no interior space for visitors, but you can walk around the base platform.
